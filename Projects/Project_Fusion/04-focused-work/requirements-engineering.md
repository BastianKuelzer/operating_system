# Requirements Engineering

**Commercial Value:**
Build as fast as possible what is actually needed.

**Problem:**
- You try to think extensively about features others already know how to build.
- You do not think about features holistically and the overall product experience becomes poor.

---

## Principles

- **Automate specifications:** Give other teams a clear guideline/template for how to provide requirements.
- **Think holistically:** Have a clear picture of what is important for the business when adding features, and how it impacts future development and the existing product.
- **Follow product prioritization:** Challenge requirements by classic product prioritization (is it aligned with our strategy? How many customers does it impact?).

---

## Requirements Template

- What is the user story?
- What pain does it solve?
- How many customers does it impact (now or potentially)?
- What are the key user stories this feature will address?
- What functional requirements are in-scope? Which are must-have vs. nice-to-have?
- Have you thought about all happy paths?
- Have you thought about all unhappy paths?
- What are the user flows?
- Do you have rough designs?
- Are there any dependencies?
- What is the timeline until it needs to be shipped?
