# ⚡ Pragmatism

**Commercial Value:**
Being pragmatic in the right context and highly diligent in another is one of the most critical skills to move fast and be ready to scale.

**Problem:**
- Taking too many shortcuts can — within weeks or months — create so many issues that you need to redo everything.
- Being too diligent means not moving fast enough, which can be fatal for a startup.

---

## Principles

1. **Start with the opinionated end in mind:** Think about the end-user's goal and work backwards from there to have a clear direction.
2. **Reuse vs. invent:** If there are proven best practices and it is not a competitive edge, do not waste time inventing something new — reuse what already exists (but understand why it's a best practice).
3. **Simple vs. Complex:** Ask yourself: what is the simplest way to achieve your goal? But you must still achieve your goal — being too pragmatic can mean you fail to deliver.
4. **Not universal:** Pragmatism is not universally good. Each context decides the level of pragmatism you should apply.

---

## Examples

1. **Start with the opinionated end in mind:**
   - Bad: Start thinking about gas cost features before thinking about the end goal (you'll spend days on part of gas costs without knowing how important it actually is).
   - Good: Look at the final gas invoice and see if it can be mapped to our electricity invoice. Start there and work backwards.

2. **Reuse vs. invent:**
   - Bad: Copy paste the portfolio management tool from a competitor (it's a core part of our value); invent a new gas cost engine (we already have one for electricity that works well enough).
   - Good: For portfolio management, design freely from user/sales perspective. For gas cost, copy the electricity structure and talk with someone who does gas invoices.

3. **Simple vs. Complex:**
   - Bad: Take electricity cost components and map them to gas, ignoring everything else (too pragmatic, misses key gas cost components).
   - Good: Look at 2–3 gas invoices to decide what level of fidelity is needed in the first gas invoice feature.

4. **Not universal:**
   - Bad: Develop the portfolio management tool and cloud migration with the same level of pragmatism (you'd need to refactor the cloud migration significantly, slowing all future engineering work).
   - Good: Vibe-code the portfolio management tool and take a more thoughtful approach to the migration to create a strong architectural basis.
