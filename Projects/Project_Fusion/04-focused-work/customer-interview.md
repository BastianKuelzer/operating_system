# Customer Interview

**Commercial Value:**
Customer interviews done incorrectly lead to wrong conclusions that lead to building no-value products, wasting entire product and tech resources. Being good at this has high commercial value.

**Problem:**
- **Leading questions:** Phrasing questions non-neutrally gives the customer a direction, so the answer doesn't reflect what they actually think.
- **No warmup:** The customer needs emotional safety to criticize you. Without it, you will not get honest feedback.
- **Talking too much:** Do not correct the customer or answer their questions — you won't learn what they actually think.

---

## Principles

### Intro
- Make explicit that critical feedback is welcome and helps you.
- Frame it as co-development: "Let's make this feature useful for you and others."
- Ask them to speak out loud what they think and what they would do before they do it. There is no right or wrong — you just want to hear their perspective.

### Show the screen
- **Set context:** In 3–4 sentences, explain what they're looking at and what goal they're trying to achieve. Do not pitch the feature or mention its value — keep it short.
- Ask them to speak out loud what they see and what they would do before doing it.
  - "What do you see there? Why? Why? Why?"
  - "Why would you use it? For what and when?"
- If possible, send the customer the prototype to click through. Otherwise, share your screen.

### Dealing with specific situations
- **Customer describes something incorrectly:** Don't correct immediately. Ask why they think that, then explain what it is and ask what they would expect.
- **Customer asks a question:** Don't answer. Ask back: "What do you think? What would you expect?"
- **Customer doesn't mention a feature you need feedback on:** You can direct them and ask specifically about it.
- **Avoid bias:** Never ask "Do you like this feature?" or "Is this good or bad?" — give no direction on whether something is positive or negative.

### Final
- "What is missing for you to make this more useful? Why? Why?"
- "Is there a question I should have asked you?"
- Thank you.
