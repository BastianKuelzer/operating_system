# Contribution Profile as Leadership Tool

**Commercial Value:**
Define targets for people that move the commercial value of the company up. People in startups are the largest leverage for this.

**Problem:**
- Setting a target that is not important for the company → it will likely not get done because resources will be pulled away and there is no attention on it.
- Setting a target that triggers internal conflict and competition → in young startups, team collaboration is key to balance constant change and avoid inefficiency.
- Setting a target where the person does not know how to execute → if confused by goals, not much will happen.
- Setting targets too low → when people are driven, they want to learn. Set targets that are slightly uncomfortable to achieve, to develop the person and generate more value for the organization.

---

## Principles

- **Align with strategy:** Ensure the profile aligns with the strategic direction of the company.
- **Pressure up/down:** Depending on the level of pressure this person needs to reach peak performance, set more or less ambitious goals. Less ambitious goals does not equal worse performance — adjust for each individual.
- **Max. amount of goals:** Max 3–4 objectives (outcome-oriented) and 2–3 sub-goals (action-oriented). Sub-goals are milestones to achieve the objective.
- **SMART goals:** Orient on SMART goals, but don't treat it as religion — deviate when needed.
  - **S**pecific — Is the goal clear and detailed?
  - **M**easurable — Can progress be tracked numerically?
  - **A**ction-oriented — Does it start with a strong verb?
  - **R**esults — What's the intended outcome?
  - **T**ime-bound — What's the timeframe for completion?
  - **e**nvironment — Consider the team's pace, pressure, and tools available.
- **Adjust goals:** Adjust goals when company priorities change — always in coordination with your team so they don't lose trust in the evaluation process.
- **Weekly check-in:** Use the contribution profile on a weekly basis to ensure work stays focused on the most important topics.
- **Agree on goals:** You can set goals without discussing with your team member once you've hit the right level of clarity and ambition, and your team perceives you as fair. It's easier to set goals together, but setting them unilaterally is sometimes the right call (e.g., when setting up a new business unit).
- **Quarter, not year:** Set goals per quarter due to the fast-changing environment.
