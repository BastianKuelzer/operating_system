# Commercialize Product — Tracking

**Commercial Value:**
Track if a product has actual commercial success or if it is just a feature we thought would add value. Be guided by commercial success to iterate on products to move the company forward.

**Problem:**
Features are developed and there is no feedback on whether they contribute to commercial success. Tracking product usage is great, but not sufficient.

---

## Principles

1. **Decision making:** For every tracking you do, calibrate which metrics to use and how to interpret them. When you look at tracking data you should be confident to make a decision — or decide to do nothing. If you cannot make a decision, your tracking is worthless.
2. **Commercial metric as end goal:** Have a commercial end goal in mind for products (new customers, renewal, cross-selling).
3. **Statistics:** Be aware of statistical significance. Testing a new feature with n=5 customers over 1 day gives no reliable conversion rate.
4. **Define proxy metrics:** If there is no clear revenue impact, tie the proxy metric to a revenue goal. Proxy metrics are product usage, NPS, CSAT.
5. **Be close when you launch:** When launching new features, have daily check-ins with commercial to get instant feedback.

---

## Examples

- **Commercial metric as end goal:**
  - Bad: We launch a feature and have no idea if there is a commercial impact.
  - Good: We launch Portfolio Management Tool and track CVR from Demo Call 2 to Closed Won.

- **Statistics:**
  - Bad: We tested with 7 customers in one day and use the % CVR as projection of commercial impact.
  - Good: We tested with 7 customers in one day and do not act on this in a major way. We wait several days and more calls before deciding.

- **Define proxy metrics:**
  - Bad: We only look at CVR as end goal for a small feature where the correlation to CVR is really small.
  - Good: We check usage of a new feature to see indications for commercial success.

- **Be close when you launch:**
  - Bad: You launch a feature and forget about it. After several weeks you check adoption.
  - Good: You talk with Sales every day at launch to clarify questions and make minor adjustments.

---

## Big bet features process

- End-KPI: CVR
- Testing period: 40 days

## Smaller features process

**At the beginning:**
- Watch PostHog recordings to iron out bigger issues
- Move to product usage — define what good usage looks like (once per month = fine, 3x per month = good, less than once per month = warning signal)
- NPS / CSAT / Very Disappointed
- Report upselling/cross-selling as part of Closed Won Reasons
- Reporting lost reasons of features

**End-KPIs:** CVR & Retention & Upselling
