# Value Proposition Canvas

**Commercial Value:**
Understanding customer pain to cluster customers and evaluate potential commercial value behind it.

**Problem:**
- Lack of deep customer understanding leads to wrong product decisions.
- Not matching customer pain points to commercial value leads to building many features that cannot sell or retain customers.

---

## Principles

1. **Context:** Before figuring out what customers need, understand the context in which a need occurs.
2. **Needs:** There are functional, social, and emotional needs. Understanding all three gives strong guidance on what the customer actually wants.
3. **Commercial:** Understanding customer needs helps you build good products. Ensuring there is commercial value lets you build a business.

---

## Examples

1. **Context:**
   - Bad: Not thinking about context at all — assuming the pain is just "seeing procurement costs," missing that the context is budget planning, which signals a specific need.
   - Good: Start with context (e.g., budget planning) and then identify the specific need.

2. **Needs:**
   - Bad: Only thinking about a feature like "show monthly procurement costs."
   - Good: Recognizing there is an emotional need for trust, which may require explaining why costs were incurred — not just showing the number.

3. **Commercial:**
   - Bad: Building a portfolio management tool for a customer who doesn't actually need it.
   - Good: Ensuring the feature is built for customers who would sign a contract because of it.

---

## Tooling

> Frameworks give you direction on how to think, but not the answer to a decision. Be ready to ignore any framework when making a decision.
