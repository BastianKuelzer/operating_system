# 🧇 Backlog Prioritization (Daily Decisions)

**Commercial Value:**
This is a key part of delivering products. The more different priorities you have, the more important it becomes to focus the team on the right things.

**Problem:**
There are too many open topics, and incorrect prioritization decisions will make the team weeks or months slower every year.

---

## Principles

- **Strategic importance:** What are the company priorities right now? What is most important for the company?
- **Satisfy, not optimize:** You will never get 100% of decisions right — no one can even define what "right" is. If the direction is correct and the majority of small decisions are good, you are doing great.
- **Urgent topics (bugs):** A customer-facing bug on a core feature is strategically important — it impacts churn and referrals. Prioritize it accordingly.
- **Developer speed:**
  - Letting engineers switch between several topics per day makes them highly inefficient.
  - Let them finish what they're on — even if something else becomes more important — if only hours or days remain. Switching will slow everything down.
- **Dependencies:** If your development blocks other teams, there is a multiplier effect on the entire org's efficiency. Even if a dependency topic is lower priority for you, consider it from a company perspective and raise its priority (depending on the other team's timeline).
- **Developer motivation:** Let engineers finish parts they're nervous about — otherwise they stay distracted worrying it might break.
- **Tech debt:** Think long term. Help engineers provide enough context to judge when tech debt will slow down velocity.
- **Usability:** Make usability part of strategic importance. If it can't be prioritized, it won't get done.
