# 🪥 Small Feature Feedback

**Commercial Value:**
Identify improvements for the customer fast.

**Problem:**
- Over-emphasizing small features at the expense of needle-moving work.
- Underestimating that many small features can have significant cumulative impact.
- Many uncoordinated small features lead to a bad product and customer experience.
- Spending too much time on small features.

---

## Principles

- **Collecting:** Gather all relevant information with minimal effort to design the feature and decide on its importance.
- **Deciding:** Ensure it aligns with company priority and that small features are part of a greater vision.
- **Communicating:** Communicate timeline, expectations, and implementation details to the internal feedback giver and the customer who requested it.

---

## Collecting

- Store all requests in a Notion database:
  - Ask for context → when does the problem occur?
  - Why do they want to change it?
  - How would they like it to look?
- Store the name of the internal feedback giver (e.g. CS)
- Store the name of the customer

## Deciding

- Use the number of customers who requested the feature for prioritization.
- Take effort/benefit ratio into account — small feature with low value but high effort → not a priority.
- Consider the overall vision for the page/area the customer wants to improve.
- Check once per week if a feature request should be moved into Linear.

## Communicating

**To internal:**
- Thank you for the feedback (encourage more)
- Give a sentence about the process (how high is the priority, etc.)
- Notify them when the feature ships

**To customers:**
- Thank them and notify them when it's released (can also go through CS — just inform CS which customer gave the feedback)

---

## Tooling

- **Notion:** Store feature requests in the requests database
- **Linear:** Store customer and internal name in the ticket for communication when done
