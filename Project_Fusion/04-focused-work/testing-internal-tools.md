# 🧪 Testing Internal Tools

**Commercial Value:**
Get tooling out as fast as possible to create value for internal teams (pre-condition: you can build it end-to-end on your own).

**Problem:**
Internal tooling product management often follows the same approach as classic software development — this is unnecessary and slows you down.

---

## Principles

1. **Be close:** If you can develop on your own and make daily progress, have frequent check-ins (several times per week) with the team you're building for — while you're building, not when you think it's done. Even a 10-minute check-in to speak out loud your assumptions, what you did, and what you'll do next is enough.
   - Risk: Internal departments often do not communicate 100% of their requirements and goals.

2. **Testing without the user:** Before showing it to the internal user, execute the sub-steps yourself to find obvious errors. No need for perfect coverage — just eliminate the most apparent issues.

3. **Testing with the user:** Have the internal user simply use the tool as it is and tell you what works and what doesn't. This creates clarity fast and prevents you from having to interpret what the internal user wants.
   - Risk: Avoid setting up formal test schemes like those used for external software. Due to being in the same organization, skip this most of the time.
